About

Tim joined CERN as a research fellow in October 2012. He currently works on the LHCb experiment which is based at CERN. He leads the team working on the reconstruction and simulation software for the upgrade Vertex Locator (VELO). He is also designing and implementing an outrageously ambitious trigger system for LHCb.

He loves to code in Python, but is OK with writing C++ (mostly for work). Look at some of his projects on: bitbucket and github. He likes to use data, simulations and statistics to find things out.

In his spare time he likes to train for and compete in triathlons. This involves swimming, biking and running crazy long distances without a break. The aim is to complete all three sports (including transitions!) in the fastest time possible.
